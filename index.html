Brown University Washing Machine
Research and Observations

Why examine the washing machine?

  • It's a public interface shared by all students of a dorm
  • It contains multiple moving parts that require different types of interactions (button, handles, trays, etc.)
  • It's used differently by each person, depending on their preferences (detergent, pod, frequency)
  • A single machine can be used by multiple people at once during turnovers

Key Observations

Preparation

  • Students do laundry when they also have other work to do so they can maximize waiting time, often on afternoons.
  • Students make separate trip to laundry room to check on washer availability prior to bringing down their laundry.
  • If none are available, students tend not to go down again for 15-30 min longer than the displayed time-left.

Engagement

  • Order of operations: loads washer, insert pod (with clothes) or detergent (in tray), configures preferences, sets timer on personal phone.
  • Number of washers used per laundry depends on frequency of use (1wk/wash -> 1 machine, 2wk/wash -> 2 machines).
  • Other factors affecting machine usage include preferences for splitting up by color, top versus bottom, and fullness per load.
